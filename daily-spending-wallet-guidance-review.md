# Oubli vs Bitcoin Design "Daily Spending Wallet" Guidance

## Short Take

Against the [Bitcoin Design daily spending wallet](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/) reference, Oubli follows the basics of a mobile self-custodial wallet reasonably well, but it does not yet match the guide's stronger patterns around contacts, metadata, backup/recovery, privacy defaults, and safe payment confirmation. Some divergence is intentional because Oubli is not a pure bitcoin-only wallet; other gaps are clear UX misses.

## Where Oubli Follows The Guidance

- Oubli clearly presents itself as self-custodial and forces the user through create-or-restore onboarding on both platforms, which matches the guide's emphasis on first-use clarity and key ownership. See [OnboardingView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/OnboardingView.swift#L80) and [OnboardingScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/OnboardingScreen.kt#L127). This aligns with the guide's [first-use](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/first-use/) direction.
- Send and Receive are primary actions on the home screen on both apps, which is consistent with the guide's preference for fast payment entry from the main screen. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L129) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L204). This matches [sending](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/sending/) and [requesting](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/requesting/).
- Oubli supports several payment entry methods the guide expects: manual entry, paste, and QR scan. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L459) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L703). That is in line with the guide's recommended [payment entry points](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/sending/).
- Both platforms have an app-level biometric barrier, which aligns with the guide's baseline wallet security model. See [LockedView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/LockedView.swift#L10) and [LockedScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/LockedScreen.kt#L30). This maps to [security](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/security/).
- Activity uses user-facing labels like `Received`, `Sent`, `Settled`, and `Emergency Exit` instead of raw event names, which is directionally aligned with the guide's advice to prefer meaning over protocol detail. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L154) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L231). This fits the spirit of [activity](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/activity/).
- Receive is now explicit on both platforms, with separate Oubli, Starknet, and Lightning modes. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L631) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L781). That is at least consistent with the guide's expectation that request flows make formats understandable.

## Where Oubli Does Not Follow The Guidance

- Oubli has no contact system. The guide treats contacts as a major simplifier for sending, requesting, and activity, but Oubli only uses raw recipient fields and hashes. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L477), [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L730), and compare that to [contacts](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/contacts/).
- Oubli also lacks metadata everywhere the guide expects it. There is no note, label, tag, payer name, recipient alias, or description on send/request, and activity has no annotations or detail view. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L448), [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L154), [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L668), and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L231). That falls short of [sending](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/sending/), [requesting](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/requesting/), and [activity](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/activity/).
- Activity is much thinner than the reference design. There is no search, filtering, export, grouping, contact linking, or transaction detail expansion. The guide explicitly calls for smart organization and search/export for heavy-use wallets. Oubli currently stops at a simple list in [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L154) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L231). Compare with [activity](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/activity/).
- Privacy only partially follows the guide. Oubli does let users hide the balance, but it is visible by default and the toggle is a tap on the whole card, with no hold-to-reveal, no PIN-to-reveal option, and no privacy settings surface. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L90) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L164). The guide's [privacy](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/privacy/) and [requesting](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/requesting/) guidance is stronger than what Oubli ships.
- Backup/recovery is materially behind the reference design. Oubli does expose the seed phrase during onboarding and supports restore from phrase, but the iOS backup flow still hard-fails and Android backup depends on preloaded state. See [OnboardingView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/OnboardingView.swift#L172), [OnboardingScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/OnboardingScreen.kt#L203), [SeedBackupView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/SeedBackupView.swift#L225), [MainScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/MainScreen.kt#L130), and [WalletViewModel.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/viewmodel/WalletViewModel.kt#L251). I also did not find user-facing cloud backup, first-funds backup reminders, or recovery of metadata/history as described in [backup & recovery](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/backup-and-recovery/landing-page/) and [recovery](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/backup-and-recovery/recovery/).
- Settings are far too thin relative to the guide. Both apps expose essentially only `Show Seed Phrase` and `Debug Settings` from the main menu. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L27) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L127). The guide expects grouped areas for privacy, security, backup, network, and help in [settings](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/settings/).
- Security is only baseline-complete. Biometrics exist, but I did not find PIN protection, security checklist/recommendations, wallet limits, or reminder flows triggered by deposits or risk changes. See [LockedView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/LockedView.swift#L36), [LockedScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/LockedScreen.kt#L80), and compare with [security](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/security/).
- Oubli blocks users inside processing states more than the guide recommends. The guide says users should not be forced to wait in a blocked interface while a transaction completes. Oubli disables dismissal during Lightning processing in send flows on both platforms. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L337) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L573). This conflicts with [sending](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/sending/).
- Receive sharing is narrower than the guide. Oubli gives QR + copy, but I did not find share sheet support, contactless sharing, metadata-rich requests, or broader request export options. See [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L887) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L846). That is weaker than [requesting](https://bitcoin.design/guide/daily-spending-wallet/requesting/).

## By-Design Differences

- The reference design is bitcoin-only and Lightning-first. Oubli is not that product. Its receive model explicitly includes `Oubli`, `Starknet`, and `Lightning`, and Lightning appears to bridge into a privacy pool rather than act as the wallet's single native balance. That is visible in [BalanceView.swift](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/ios/Oubli/Views/BalanceView.swift#L677) and [BalanceScreen.kt](/Users/adrienlacombe/ws/oubli/android/app/src/main/java/com/oubli/wallet/ui/BalanceScreen.kt#L846). So some mismatch is strategic, not necessarily wrong, but it means Oubli should not assume the reference design fully fits without adaptation.

## Bottom Line

Oubli follows the guide on core wallet scaffolding: self-custody messaging, home-screen send/receive, biometric lock, and basic payment entry. It falls short on the parts that make a daily-spending wallet feel mature and trustworthy in daily use: safe invoice import, contacts, metadata, richer activity, privacy defaults, reliable backup/recovery, and a real settings/security surface.
